Thread: "Honest dining hall tier list - which is actually worth going to?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (847 upvotes):
Okay here's my honest ranking after 3 years eating on campus:

1. Rams Head - best food quality, least crowded, but out of the way if you're not near south campus. The pasta station is actually good. Has the best salad bar on campus. Seating is never a problem after 1pm.

2. Top of Lenoir - most variety by far but get there before 11:30 or after 1:15 or you'll wait 20+ minutes just to get in. The stir fry station has gotten better. Avoid the pizza, it's sad. The dessert station is underrated.

3. Chase - convenient if you live in Craige or Hinton James but the food quality is noticeably worse. Gets extremely loud during peak hours. The breakfast is actually their best meal - worth going early. Dinner is often leftovers from lunch repackaged.

4. Rams Head Dining Hall (not Rams Head Café) - I know it's confusing. This one is fine but closes early on weekends. Limited vegan options compared to Top of Lenoir.

Reply (312 upvotes):
Chase breakfast is genuinely slept on. 8-9am is the sweet spot - food is fresh, it's not crowded, and the omelet station actually has someone staffing it. By 10am it becomes a madhouse and quality drops off.

Reply (201 upvotes):
Hot take: all the dining halls are roughly the same at 11pm. If you need late night food, Top of Lenoir and Chase both stay open later than Rams Head. The late-night menus are limited (mostly sandwiches, cereal, leftover hot food) but it's something.

Reply (156 upvotes):
The allergen labeling at Top of Lenoir has gotten much better. If you have dietary restrictions, use the UNC Dining app - you can filter by allergen. Rams Head has the best gluten-free options. Chase improved their vegan station this year.

Thread: "Is the meal plan worth it as a freshman?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (534 upvotes):
Freshmen living in residence halls are required to have a meal plan. Given that, here's what I wish I knew:

- Meal swipes don't roll over week to week, so don't let them accumulate. Use them all.
- Dining dollars DO roll over semester to semester but expire at end of the year. Spend them at the on-campus restaurants and cafes, not just the dining halls.
- The residential dining halls are crowded 12-1:30pm M-F. Avoid this window or go off-peak.
- Lenoir has the best selection on weekends but reduced hours. Check the UNC Dining website for holiday schedules.
- Swipe your card at one of the smaller cafes (like Ram's Den) when you're in a rush - shorter lines.

Reply (289 upvotes):
The meal plan is definitely more expensive than cooking yourself but freshman year you probably don't have a kitchen. The social aspect of going to Lenoir with your hall is actually nice. Just don't expect gourmet food. It's cafeteria food. Manage expectations and you'll be fine.

Yelp Review - Top of Lenoir (3.5/5 average)

Review by grad student:
"Lenoir is the main dining hub and it shows - it's always packed between noon and 1:30. The food quality is inconsistent. Some days the hot entrees are genuinely good, other days everything tastes like it's been sitting out for hours. The salad bar is usually fresh. The smoothie station has a long line but the smoothies are worth it. Parking nearby is impossible so don't try to drive. It's connected to the student union area so grab coffee at Top of the Hill (the campus coffee shop, not the bar) afterward."

Review by sophomore:
"Went vegan this semester and honestly the vegan options at Lenoir are better than I expected. They clearly label everything and there's always at least one vegan hot entree. The tofu scramble at breakfast is legitimately good. My non-vegan friends complain the food is boring but I've found it totally workable. Just go at off-peak times."
