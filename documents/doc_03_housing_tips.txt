Thread: "Comprehensive guide to off-campus housing near UNC - what nobody tells you" (r/UNC, 1.2k upvotes)

Top comment:
After two years of renting near campus I want to share everything I wish I knew. This is long but all of it matters.

AREAS TO KNOW:

Carrboro (west of campus): The most popular area for upperclassmen. Franklin Street extends west into Carrboro and you're still walkable to campus. Rents are slightly lower than Chapel Hill proper. The neighborhood has good restaurants, the Cat's Cradle music venue, and a very chill vibe. Downside: some apartments are older and haven't been updated since the 90s. Always check for mold in bathrooms and under sinks.

Northside (north of campus): Very close to Kenan Stadium and the gym. Mostly residential, quieter than Carrboro. Some beautiful old houses rented by the room. Downsides: parking is nightmarish and the streets flood during heavy rain. If you're renting a house with a basement, ask specifically about flooding history.

Southern Village / Ephesus: Further from campus (10-15 min drive or bus). Much newer construction, modern amenities, quieter. Better for grad students or upperclassmen who have a car. Bus service exists but it's not frequent on weekends.

LANDLORD AND LEASE TIPS:

Reply (678 upvotes):
The three worst landlord companies in Chapel Hill that you should research carefully before signing:

- Orange County reality management has had persistent maintenance complaint threads here every semester. People report delays of weeks for basic repairs.
- Some private landlords on Craigslist will try to charge you for normal wear and tear when you move out. Always do a move-in photo walkthrough and send photos to the landlord by email to create a paper trail.
- Be very careful about any lease with a "noise clause" - some landlords use these to withhold security deposits for normal activity.

ALWAYS do the following before signing:
1. Walk the unit yourself - don't sign based on online photos
2. Run hot water in all faucets and flush the toilet
3. Check window seals and any exterior walls for mold or water damage
4. Ask current tenants (if any) what the landlord is like
5. Google the landlord name + "Chapel Hill reviews" before signing anything

Reply (445 upvotes):
The UNC Off-Campus Housing office has a listing board that's actually useful. It's less scammy than Craigslist because landlords have to register to post. You can find it on the UNC housing website. They also have free lease review services where a law student will look at your lease - USE THIS, it has saved people thousands of dollars.

Thread: "what are utilities like for off-campus apartments?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (312 upvotes):
Utilities vary wildly. Here's what I've seen:

- Electricity in older Chapel Hill buildings can be brutal in summer (AC running constantly) and winter (poor insulation). Budget $80-120/month per person for a 2BR in an old building, less for new construction.
- Most apartments include water. If yours doesn't, budget $30-40/person/month.
- Internet: AT&T fiber is available in most of Chapel Hill now. Spectrum is also available. Budget $60-80/month split between roommates.
- Total utility cost: expect $100-150/person/month on top of rent in an older unit. Newer buildings are often more energy efficient.

Reply (201 upvotes):
The most underrated cost: renter's insurance. It's like $15/month and covers your laptop, furniture, and clothes if there's a break-in or fire. Your parents' homeowners insurance might cover you but verify before assuming. UNC's student legal services can advise on this.

Thread: "Housing lottery tips for staying on campus sophomore year" (r/UNC)

Top comment (289 upvotes):
The housing lottery is NOT random. Your selection time is determined by when you submit your housing application (not the lottery itself) and by your seniority/credits. Here's what actually matters:

- Submit your housing application THE MINUTE it opens. Being early in the application window gives you an earlier selection time.
- Pick roommates who also submitted early - your group's selection time is based on the latest submitter in your group.
- Know which dorms you want BEFORE your selection time - you won't have time to research during the selection.
- If you miss a dorm you wanted, check back the next day - there are always cancellations.
- Craige Tower rooms are larger than they look in photos. Ram Village apartments are popular but fill within hours.
- Granville Towers is technically off-campus but acts like a dorm. It has more amenities (pool, gym) but you're in a different parking zone.
