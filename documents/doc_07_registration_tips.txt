Thread: "ConnectCarolina course registration survival guide" (r/UNC, 1.5k upvotes)

Top comment:
Registration at UNC is competitive and the system is ancient. Here is everything that works:

BEFORE YOUR REGISTRATION TIME:
1. Build your shopping cart before your registration window opens. You cannot add courses to your cart during another student's registration window but you CAN pre-load them. Do this the night before.
2. Have a list of backup courses ready. Know exactly what section and number you want, not just the course name.
3. Be logged into ConnectCarolina at least 5 minutes before your time. The system can be slow on high-traffic mornings.
4. Have a second browser tab open as backup. If one tab freezes, submit from the other.

COMMON TRAPS:
- Prerequisites aren't always enforced automatically. You may be able to enroll in a course you don't meet the prereqs for - but the professor can drop you. Check prereqs yourself.
- "Full" sections sometimes open up after the first week of classes when people drop. Keep checking.
- Waitlists exist for some courses and not others. If a course doesn't have a formal waitlist, email the professor directly. "I am very interested in your course and am on the waitlist" emails actually work sometimes.
- Honors sections sometimes have separate enrollment. If you're in the Honors Carolina program, make sure you're enrolling in the right section.

Reply (678 upvotes):
The best kept secret: professors of small seminars and writing-intensive courses have discretionary enrollment. They can add students above the cap. A polite, specific email to the professor explaining why you want to take the course can genuinely work. Don't send a generic email - mention something specific about the course topic or how it fits your academic goals.

Reply (512 upvotes):
General Education requirements (the "GenEd" requirements) are easier to fulfill than people think but you need to plan ahead. Check the approved course list early - many interesting courses count for multiple GenEd requirements. Courses that count for both a major requirement and a GenEd requirement are gold. The Registrar's page has the full list.

Reply (389 upvotes):
Registering for graduate courses as an undergrad: it is possible with permission. You need instructor approval and usually department approval. These courses can count as major electives with advisor approval. Worth pursuing if you're interested in going to grad school - it signals preparation and gives you something to discuss in applications.

Thread: "What's the academic calendar for UNC and what dates actually matter?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (334 upvotes):
Dates nobody tells you until it's too late:

- Add/Drop deadline (end of first week): You can drop a course without any record of it. After this, a drop shows as "W" on your transcript. Know this deadline.
- Late withdrawal deadline (usually around week 10-11): After this point you cannot withdraw from a course at all without a hardship petition (which requires documentation).
- Grade change deadline: If you believe there's a grading error, you typically have until the end of the following semester to appeal.
- Incomplete grade deadline: If you get an "IN" (incomplete), you must complete the work within a specific window or it converts to an F.

FINALS SCHEDULE:
Finals are NOT always during the last week of class. The official finals schedule is posted mid-semester. Some finals are during the designated exam period; some professors hold finals during the last class session. Know which situation applies to each of your courses. The registrar's office posts the exam schedule.

Thread: "Double major at UNC - is it actually worth it?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (267 upvotes):
Speaking from experience doing CS + Statistics double major:

Worth it if:
- The two majors have significant overlap in required courses
- You're genuinely interested in both, not just doing it for optics
- You have the credits (can graduate in 4 years without overloading)

Not worth it if:
- You're adding a second major just to look more competitive (employers care more about internships and projects)
- The majors have no overlap and you'd need 5 years to finish
- It means you can't take any electives or explore other interests

The Stats + CS combination is actually quite efficient - many stats courses count toward CS electives and vice versa. Data Science students can often complete it. Check with both advisors before committing because the course sequencing requires careful planning.
