Thread: "Everything you need to know about getting around UNC campus" (r/UNC, 567 upvotes)

Top comment:
Transportation at UNC is something nobody explains well. Here's everything:

CAMPUS BUSES (FREE with OneCard):
- UNC operates a free bus system called the TRANSIT. It connects all major parts of campus including South Campus, Manning Drive (medical area), and Rams Head.
- The J, CW, NS, and other routes cover different areas. Download the GoTriangle Transit app or the TransLoc app to see real-time bus locations.
- Buses run frequently on weekdays (every 5-15 min on major routes) but are sparse on weekends (every 30+ minutes on some routes). Plan accordingly.
- The night owl routes run until 2am on weekends.

CHAPEL HILL TRANSIT (FREE with UNC ID):
- This is the city bus system, separate from the UNC internal system. It connects campus to downtown Chapel Hill, Carrboro, and nearby areas.
- The CW (Chapel Hill-Carrboro route) is heavily used by students going to Franklin Street.
- GoTriangle connects Chapel Hill to Durham, RDU airport, and Raleigh. This costs money (though discounted with UNC ID).

PARK AND RIDE:
- If you have a car, don't pay for a campus parking pass. Get a Park & Ride permit (much cheaper, I think around $120/year vs $800+ for campus).
- P&R lots are on the periphery. Shuttles run frequently to campus during the week.
- Parking on campus without a permit will get you ticketed fast. Parking enforcement is aggressive.

Reply (389 upvotes):
Biking around campus: the campus is hilly. Very hilly. Coming from a flat place you will be surprised. Electric bikes have become very popular because of the hills. If you bike, get a quality U-lock (Kryptonite or similar) - bike theft happens regularly in high-traffic areas. Register your bike with UNC Campus Police - if it's recovered after theft, it can be returned to you.

Reply (278 upvotes):
Getting to RDU Airport (Raleigh-Durham): options from cheapest to most expensive:
1. GoTriangle Route 100 bus: $3 (with UNC ID discount), takes about 60-90 minutes with transfers. Look up the route in advance.
2. Rideshare (Uber/Lyft): ~$25-40 depending on time of day. Split with friends if you're traveling at similar times.
3. UNC affiliates sometimes organize carpool groups during holiday breaks - check the UNC ride share group on Facebook.

Reply (201 upvotes):
Enterprise and Hertz have car rental locations in Chapel Hill and near campus. For longer trips or big moves, renting is cheaper than you think. Zipcar is also available on campus - you pay hourly, useful for grocery runs or occasional trips without owning a car.

Thread: "What's the deal with UNC campus parking?" (r/UNC)

Top comment (412 upvotes):
Campus parking is a running joke at UNC. Here's the reality:

The university has many more parking permits issued than parking spaces (this is intentional and based on statistical use patterns). In practice this means:
- During peak hours (9am-3pm weekdays) lots near academic buildings are full.
- Evenings and weekends are much easier.
- Many people with permits end up parking in a lot different from the one they paid for.

PERMIT TYPES:
- Commuter: most common for commuting students. Specific lot.
- Park and Ride: lower cost, shuttle to campus.
- Resident: for students who live on campus or nearby.
- Medical: near medical complex.

TICKETS:
- UNC parking tickets are not jokes. They're $50+ per violation and they will boot your car after multiple unpaid tickets.
- If you get a ticket you believe was wrong, you CAN appeal within 14 days. The appeal process is online and sometimes works if you have a legitimate case.
- Tickets go to collections if unpaid for too long and can affect your ability to register for classes. Take them seriously.
