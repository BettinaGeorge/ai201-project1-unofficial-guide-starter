Professor: David Stotts (COMP 283 - Discrete Mathematics, COMP 550 - Algorithms)
Rating: 4.2/5

Review 1:
Stotts is a genuinely caring professor who wants every student to succeed. COMP 283 (Discrete Math) is a course that intimidates a lot of CS students but he makes the material approachable. He explains proofs slowly and carefully, checks for understanding during lecture, and gives plenty of practice problems. If you go to office hours he will work through problems with you from the beginning. Very patient.

Review 2:
Took COMP 550 (Algorithms) with Stotts. This is a hard course no matter who teaches it but he structures it well. Dynamic programming is where most people struggle - he goes over it multiple times in different ways. His exams are proof and analysis heavy. You need to be comfortable writing formal algorithm correctness proofs. The textbook (CLRS) is the standard algorithms text and he follows it closely.

Review 3:
His COMP 283 course saved my CS career. I was seriously considering switching majors after failing the first midterm. He noticed I was struggling and reached out - actual professor reaching out to a student. He connected me with tutoring resources and adjusted how he explained certain concepts in office hours. Rare level of personal investment from a professor at a large university.

Review 4:
Stotts posts detailed lecture notes before every class. His slides are clear and well-organized. He does live coding demos for the algorithmic content which helps make abstract ideas concrete. Homework is assigned weekly in COMP 550 and takes 3-6 hours depending on the week. Don't fall behind - the material builds on itself rapidly. Graph algorithms week is the hardest.

Review 5:
COMP 283 with Stotts: attendance really matters here. He covers material in lecture that doesn't appear in the textbook, and his exam questions often reflect specific examples from class. He gives pop quizzes occasionally to incentivize attendance. They're low stakes individually but add up. His grading is generous if you show your work and reasoning even when you get the wrong answer.

Professor: David Stotts (General)
Rating: 4.3/5

Review 6:
One of the most approachable full professors in the department. He actually remembers students' names which is remarkable in a department this size. His enthusiasm for theoretical CS is contagious even if you came in thinking theory was boring. He has a way of connecting abstract math concepts to practical programming problems that makes both feel more meaningful.

Review 7:
For COMP 550, practice exam problems from previous semesters - he posts them on Sakai. The exam format is consistent year to year. Master dynamic programming, greedy algorithms, and graph traversal (BFS/DFS, Dijkstra, Bellman-Ford). Know your time complexity analysis cold. If you can explain why an algorithm is correct AND analyze its runtime, you'll do well on his exams.
